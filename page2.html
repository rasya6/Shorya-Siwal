Festival Foods


Appetizers Main Dishes Desserts
Samosa: A crispy and spicy samosa is the perfect snack for this festive occasion. Biryani: A mixed rice dish which is made with Indian spices, rice, and usually some type of meat. Rice Kheer: An Indian rice pudding made with basmati rice, whole milk, sugar, nuts and spices
Aloo Bonda: This streetside snack from South India contains delicious potato stuffing inside the golden crispy coating of gram flour. Kadhai Paneer: A popular Indian paneer dish made with paneer, onions, and bell peppers cooked in a spicy onion tomato gravy with masala, usually served with roti Gulab Jamun: Khoya balls dipped in thick sugar syrup
Murukku: A popular South Indian snack which is made of rice flour, and can be made at home with simple ingredients and minimum effort. Dal Makhani & Jeera Rice: A soft, consistent lentil paste that has a creamy and smooth flavor, served with Jeera Rice Soan Papdi: A cube-shaped dessert with a crisp and flaky texture
Onion Bhaji: Deep-fried crispy and spicy snacks made with onions, gram flour and spices, which is usually served alongside hot tea or coffee. Achari Aloo: A Punjabi potato dish made using Indian pickling spices, usually served with Paratha. Gulab Jamun: The national dessert of India made from a khoya milk dough
Paneer Tikka: Soft Paneer cubes with a flavorful spicy sauce. Pindi Chole: A chickpea dish made in Punjabi style with chole masala powder, usually served with butter naan. Batasha: An Indian sweet candy made of refined sugar


Festival Activities


  • Edible diya craft: Children can make diya designs on top of biscuits.
  • Rangoli sketching and painting: Create beautiful rangoli on the floor and fill them with beautiful colours.
  • Glitter Diwali cards: Create Diwali Cards for your loved ones with glitter.
  • Candle Making: Create Candles for Diwali.
  • Lantern Making: Create Lanterns as decoration for Diwali.
  • Jewelry Making: Create jewelry with colorful gemstones for Diwali.
